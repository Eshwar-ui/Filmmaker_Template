Documentary Filmmaker

Marcus Holloway

Award-winning documentary filmmaker based in New York, specializing in human rights, environmental, and cultural documentaries. 15 years of bringing untold stories to light.

Marcus Holloway is an independent documentary filmmaker whose work focuses on human rights, environmental justice, and the untold stories of marginalized communities. Born and raised in Brooklyn, New York, Marcus discovered his passion for visual storytelling at the age of sixteen when he borrowed a Hi8 camcorder and documented the daily lives of workers at a local bodega.

After graduating from NYU Tisch School of the Arts with a degree in Film & Television, Marcus spent three years as a camera operator for National Geographic before founding his own production company, Holloway Films. His debut feature documentary, "Dispatch" (2020), followed three war correspondents across two continents and premiered at the Tribeca Film Festival, where it won the Grand Jury Prize.

His films have screened at over 40 international festivals, including Sundance, IDFA, Hot Docs, and the Venice Film Festival. Marcus is a two-time Cinema Eye Honors nominee and a recipient of the International Documentary Association's Emerging Filmmaker Award. When not in the field, he mentors aspiring filmmakers through the Tribeca Film Institute's documentary program.

My Approach

A Philosophy of Immersion

"I believe documentary filmmaking is an act of listening. My camera is not a voyeur — it's a witness. I spend months, sometimes years, embedded with my subjects because the most honest stories can only emerge when trust replaces performance. I chase the quiet moments, the pauses between words, the glances that reveal what language cannot."
